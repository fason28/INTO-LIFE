# Movement and Camera Feel - Into Life

## Overall Goal
Movement should feel natural, calm, and realistic — like a real person walking in everyday life.  
Not too fast like an action game, not too slow like a walking simulator.

## First-Person Camera Rules
- Mouse/finger look should be smooth and responsive.
- Looking up and down should feel natural (can look at the sky or at feet).
- When turning around to look at the school building (after exiting), the camera movement must feel meaningful and emotional.
- No head bobbing at the beginning (we can add gentle bobbing later if it feels good).

## Walking Feel
- Normal walking speed: comfortable and relaxed.
- Player can walk freely in any direction.
- No running in the very first prototype (running can be added later).
- Jumping should be simple and optional (mainly for testing).

## Important Moments
- During graduation exit: Player walks down stairs/aisle at normal speed.
- After exiting building: Player can freely turn 360 degrees and look up at the entire school building from bottom to top. This moment should feel special.
- At the school gate and beyond: Player explores at their own pace.

## Future Improvements (Later)
- Gentle footstep sounds when walking.
- Very light head bobbing while walking.
- Different movement speeds (normal walk vs slightly faster when in hurry).
- Mobile touch controls (virtual joystick + look) — must feel good on phone.

## Development Priority
1. Make basic WASD + mouse look feel good first (on PC).
2. Make first-person building look moment feel meaningful.
3. Only then improve with animations, sounds, and mobile support.
