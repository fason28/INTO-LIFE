# First Interactions - Into Life

## Purpose
Define the very first things the player can interact with after the "Welcome Into Life" screen, keeping everything extremely simple for the initial prototype.

## Location
Small outdoor area just outside the school gate.

## Available Interactions (First Prototype - Very Limited)

1. **Notice Board / Sign**
   - Simple static object near the road.
   - When player gets close and presses interaction key (E or tap on mobile):
     - Shows neutral advice text: "Many young people look for their first job here."
   - No actual jobs yet — just information.

2. **Basic Buildings in Distance**
   - A few placeholder buildings (simple boxes with textures later):
     - One labeled "Hostel"
     - One labeled "Internet Café"
     - One labeled "Town Center"
   - When player approaches a building:
     - Text appears: "This is the local hostel. You might be able to rent a room here if you have money."

3. **Gate Keeper (Already Planned)**
   - Gives initial advice when player reaches the gate.

4. **Simple Objects**
   - A bench where player can sit (optional, just for testing movement).
   - A trash bin or street sign (purely decorative for now).

## Interaction Style
- No menus that force choices.
- Player must walk close to objects to trigger text or dialogue.
- All advice is neutral and informative only.
- Player decides what to do next (explore, walk toward a building, etc.).

## Technical Notes for Prototype
- Use Area3D or simple distance check for interaction.
- Show text on screen (Label or RichTextLabel).
- Keep interactions very basic — no complex UI yet.

## Future Expansion
- Real job listings on notice board
- Entering buildings (hostel, café, university office)
- Talking to NPCs
- Seeing other players

## Goal for First Prototype
Player should feel they can freely explore and discover things on their own, with gentle guidance only.
